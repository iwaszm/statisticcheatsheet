💡 Key Assumptions Dictionary

Understanding the prerequisites for your statistical models is crucial to avoid false positives/negatives. Review core terminology here.

Independence (独立性)

Data points do not influence each other. A person's score does not affect another's. Violations here usually require Mixed Models (LMM) or Repeated Measures.

Normality of residuals (残差正态性)

The difference between observed and predicted values should be normally distributed. Often checked with Q-Q plots or Shapiro-Wilk test.

Homogeneity of variance (方差齐性)

Also called homoscedasticity. The variance within each group (or along the regression line) should be roughly equal. Note: Welch's t-test bypasses this.

Sphericity (球形假设)

Only for repeated measures (rmANOVA). The variances of the differences between all possible pairs of within-subject conditions are equal. Mixed Models (LMM) bypasses this.

Multicollinearity (多重共线性)

In regression, predictors (IVs) should not be too highly correlated with each other, as it inflates variance and makes coefficient estimates unstable. Checked via VIF (Variance Inflation Factor).
